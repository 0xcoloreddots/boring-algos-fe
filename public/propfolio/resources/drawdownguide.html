Section 1

What drawdown actually means
for a positive expectancy system.

A positive expectancy system means that over a large enough sample of trades, the system makes money. Not every trade. Not every week. Not even every month. But over time, across hundreds of trades, the edge compounds in your favour.

Drawdown is what happens in between. It is the natural variance of any system with real edge. Every single year of our backtesting contained drawdown periods. The strategies survived them all and remained profitable. What you are seeing right now is within the expected distribution of outcomes. We constantly monitor the live maximum drawdowns of all our Propfolios and if they ever breach our threshold we will inform you immediately to pause those Propfolios.

The system is proven to make money over time. One drawdown period does not change that.

Section 2

Prop firm expectancy.
The same logic, one level up.

In trading, you do not know if the next trade will be a win or a loss. But if your system has positive expectancy, you know that over a large enough sample of trades you will be net positive. That is what makes a trading system worth running.

Prop firm challenges work the same way. We do not know if the next challenge we run will pass or not. And honestly, we do not need to know. What we do know is that over a large sample of challenge attempts, our Propfolios are net positive. Some will pass, some will not, but the overall result across many attempts is profitable.

Just like in trading, one losing trade does not define your system's edge — one failed challenge does not define ours. The edge is defined by hundreds of trades and multiple challenge cycles over time. You are looking at one data point in a much larger sample.

We know our Propfolios have positive expectancy at both the trade level and the challenge level because of our rigorous backtesting framework — 6 years in-sample, 1 year out-of-sample, Monte Carlo simulation, parameter sensitivity analysis, and live forward testing. The data supports running the system through drawdown, not stopping it.

Section 3

Why uncorrelated strategies and portfolios
protect you during drawdown.

This is exactly why we built Propfolio the way we did, and why we recommend running multiple Propfolios rather than just one.

Uncorrelated strategies inside a portfolio means that when one strategy is in drawdown, the others are not moving in the same direction. They carry the account while the underperforming strategy recovers. The result: lower overall drawdown with the same or better returns compared to running a single strategy alone.

Low-correlation portfolios takes this one level further. Each Propfolio (A, B, C) has low correlation with the others. When you run multiple Propfolios across separate challenge accounts, you get portfolios that each have low drawdown and strong returns — so your overall chance of passing at least one challenge in any given period is significantly higher than if you were running one account with one approach.

This is the core reason we recommend having multiple Propfolios running simultaneously. It is not just about diversification for its own sake. It is a deliberate strategy to increase your probability of getting funded and getting payouts across any given time period.

04

Do not close any open trades manually

This is the most common and most costly mistake. Closing trades manually locks in losses and removes the system's ability to recover. The algo placed those trades with a defined exit logic. Let it execute. The moment you manually close a trade, you have taken over from the system.

Do not do this
05

Do not change any settings or risk parameters

Reducing lot sizes mid-challenge changes the strategy's expectancy. Increasing them to recover losses faster is even more dangerous. The settings were configured for your specific prop firm's rules. Leave them exactly as they are.

Do not do this
06

Verify the algos are still running correctly

Check that your VPS is online and your MT4/MT5 terminal is connected. Check that the EA is enabled on each chart and showing as active. If anything looks wrong technically, refer to your troubleshooting guide or message Sam.
